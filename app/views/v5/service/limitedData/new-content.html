Importing Pinus thunbergia Parl. from Japan

Prohibition information

! Warning You must not import Pinus thunbergia Parl. from Japan as plants for planting.

Even with import restrictions, you may, in special cases, be able to apply for authorisation to import your goods.

Prohibition exception

The prohibition does not apply to:

  • naturally and artificially dwarfed plants for planting (either entirely of the species Pinus pentaphylla Sieb. & Zucc., or of Pinus pentaphylla Sieb & Zucc. grafted on a rootstock of a Pinus species other than Pinus pentaphylla Sieb. & Zucc.)
  • seeds

Prohibition details

There are other special measures that apply to naturally and artificially dwarfed plants for planting.

Import rules

Naturally and artificially dwarfed plants for planting or seeds

To import Pinus thunbergia Parl. from Japan in the above formats, ​you will need to :

  • register to import if you're a first-time importer
  • get a phytosanitary certificate from the National Plant Protection Organisation of the exporting country
  • meet the ISPM 15 international standard for any wood packaging material you use
  • notify the relevant authorities about your import
  • comply with documentary, identity and physical checks at the border
  • pay fees, which will include plant health checks
The phytosanitary certificate may need an additional declaration. To find out, check the additional declaration tool.

Pests and diseases you must report

Different formats of Pinus thunbergia Parl might carry the pests or diseases from Japan listed at the bottom of the page. These pests and diseases are considered a risk to Great Britain's agriculture, horticulture, wildlife or forestry. If one of these pests or diseases is found during plant health inspections, action will be taken. This could result in consignments being delayed or destroyed.

You must report any suspicions of these pests or diseases to the relevant authority.
