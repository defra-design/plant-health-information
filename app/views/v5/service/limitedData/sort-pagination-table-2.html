Plants for planting

! Warning You must not import {{data['plantName']}} from {{data['country']}} in the above format unless they are:
  • bonsai
  • invitro material
  • 1 to 3 year old bare-rooted, dormant, without leaves, grafted or budded plants for planting

Despite import restrictions, you may be able to  apply for authorisation to import your plant or plant product.

Bonsai, invitro material or 1 to 3 year old bare-rooted, dormant, without leaves, grafted or budded plants for planting

To import {{data['plantName']}} from {{data['country']}} in the above formats, you will need to (the following links open in a new tab):

  • register to import if you're importing for the first time
  • get a phytosanitary certificate from your exporter
  • meet the ISPM 15 international standard for any wood packaging material you use
  • notify the relevant authorities about your import
  • comply with documentary, identity and physical checks at the border
  • pay fees for plant health checks
You may need to include an additional declaration on the phytosanitary certificate. To find out, check the additional declaration tool (opens in a new tab).

Pests and diseases you must report

Different formats of {{data['plantName']}} might carry the pests or diseases from {{data['country']}} listed at the bottom of the page. These pests and diseases are considered a risk to Great Britain's agriculture, horticulture or wildlife. If one of these pests or diseases is found during plant health inspections, action will be taken. This could result in consignments being delayed or destroyed.

You must report any suspicions of these pests or diseases to the relevant authority

You can find out how often inspections at the border have resulted in the destruction of goods (opens in a new tab).
