Reporting suspicions of Agrilus bilineatus

You only need to report sightings or suspicions ​of Agrilus bilineatus if found on the following plant-format combinations within your supply chain network. You do not need to report this pest if you find it on other plant-format combinations.  ​ If Agrilus bilineatus​ is found on any of the listed plant-format combinations during routine plant health inspections, you will be asked to take action.

Scientific name Common names
Castanea dentata American chestnut
Quercus alba eastern white oak, white oak
Quercus coccinea scarlet oak
Quercus ellipsoidalis Hill's oak, jack oak, northern pin oak
Quercus lyrata overcup oak, water white oak
Quercus macrocarpa bur oak, mossy-cup oak, scrub oak
Quercus marilandica blackjack oak
Quercus michauxii basket oak, cow oak, swamp chestnut oak
Quercus muehlenbergii chinkapin oak, chinquapin oak, yellow chestnut oak
Quercus nigra water oak

Other plants known to carry Agrilus bilineatus

Agrilus bilineatus can also be found on the following plants. You do not need to report any sightings or suspicions if found on these plants.

Scientific name Common names
Quercus palustris pin oak, swamp oak
Quercus prinus basket oak, chestnut oak
Quercus robur common oak, English oak
Quercus rubra American red oak, northern red oak, red oak
Quercus shumardii var. texana Nuttall's oak, southern red oak, Texas oak, Texas red oak
Quercus stellata post oak
Quercus velutina black oak, dyer's oak, eastern black oak
Quercus virginiana live oak, southern live oak
