Prohibition information

! Warning You must not import {{data['plantName']}} from {{data['country']}} as {{data['format']}}.

Even with import restrictions, you may, in special cases, be able to apply for authorisation to import your goods.

Prohibition exceptions

The prohibition does not apply to:

  • in vitro material
  • naturally or artificially dwarfed {{data['format']}}
  • 1 to 3 year old bare-rooted, dormant, without leaves, grafted or budded plants for planting
  • seeds

Import rules

In vitro material, naturally or artificially dwarfed plants for planting or 1 to 3 year old bare-rooted, dormant, without leaves, grafted or budded plants for planting

To import {{data['plantName']}} from {{data['country']}} in the above formats, you will need to:

  • register to import if you're a first-time importer
  • get a phytosanitary certificate from the National Plant Protection Organisation of the exporting country
  • meet the ISPM 15 international standard for any wood packaging material you use
  • notify the relevant authorities about your import
  • comply with documentary, identity and physical checks at the border
  • pay fees, which will include​ plant health checks
The phytosanitary certificate may need an additional declaration. To find out, check the additional declaration tool.

Seeds

To import {{data['plantName']}} from {{data['country']}} in the above format, you will need to:

  • get a phytosanitary certificate from the National Plant Protection Organisation of the exporting country
  • meet the ISPM 15 international standard for any wood packaging material you use
The phytosanitary certificate may need an additional declaration. To find out, check the additional declaration tool.

You can find out how often inspections at the border have resulted in the destruction of goods.

Pests and diseases you must report

Different formats of {{data['plantName']}} might carry the pests or diseases from {{data['country']}} listed at the bottom of the page. These pests and diseases are considered a risk to Great Britain's agriculture, horticulture, wildlife or forests. If one of these pests or diseases is found during plant health inspections, action will be taken. This could result in consignments being delayed or destroyed.

You must report any suspicions of these pests or diseases to the relevant authority.
